WHAT THE FUTURE HOLDS FOR AN AI & ANALYTICS STUDENT

Artificial Intelligence and Analytics - a sector which undergoes constant technological advancements and innovations. No doubt, it is highly competitive and extremely demanding. Many students may have the initial interest in embarking on a journey on artificial intelligence and data analytics, but find themselves unable to cope with the rigours of the curriculum.

Even the workforce is a competitive arena, especially in high demand jobs - such as : Software Engineers and Application Developers amongst many others. The future of an AI student is often bright with a plethora of enriching opportunties, but these opportunities would only materialise to those who are able to take advantage and seize it at the right moment.

APPLICATION DEVELOPER

As an Application Developer, I would be at the forefront of creating new applications as well as maintaining apps.

Based on my research, I can conclude that the job scope is rather broad and can include building of softwares to meet client needs, as well as keeping up with the lifecycle of the application to make sure there are consistent updates to increase the performance of the application.

Personally, I am invested in creating new softwares that can meet certain needs in the community, such as recycling apps or transport applications. One such project that I am working on involves the building of an application using JavaScript to cater to shoppers membership loyalty.

I chose this as one of my career pathways as I am passionate about design and the principles behind it, and want to build a software to be able to contribute positively to the society.

DATA ANALYST

Taking up the job of a Data Analyst is undoubtedly not an easy feat. As a data analyst, I understand that the job involves predicting and analysing future trends with past occurences to be used as a basis.

Data analysts can be said to mine data to better understand the past, and use data to make assumptions about the future. Data analysts examine data sets with the intention of drawing insights about things that have happened, and presenting coherent stories through visualizations. (Jenny Scribani, LightHouse Labs).

Hence, being inquisitive and analytical in nature, I am very interested in studying data patterns and forming conclusions from the information gathered. Through these experiences, I aspire to effectively leverage on data analytics, coupled with machine learning algorithms to detect hidden patterns, especially for the pattern correlating to increased identity thefts.

To gain more knowledge in this industry, I am actively partaking in data analytics workshops and hackathons to find out about this exciting industry.

AI DEVOPS ENGINEER

Another career option I'm thoroughly interested in is being an AI DevOps Engineer. It comprises of working with both artificial intelligence and engineering of products.

But what is it all about? I will be responsible for the designing of emerging technologies and automate such solutions using Machine Learning. Without AI, a DevOps Engineer has the sole responsibility of reviewing and validating source codes in the software and troubleshooting any programming errors.

However, there is now greater integration of AI systems into the job of a DevOps engineer, thus softwares need to be interactive instead of remaining static.

As such, I'm also working towards developing the necessary skills and aptitude to meet the job requirements, such as pursuing a diploma in Applied Artificial Intelligence and spending time to pick up the principles of engineering and design to better relate to the job.
