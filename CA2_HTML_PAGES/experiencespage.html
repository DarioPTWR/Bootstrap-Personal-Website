COURSE EXPERIENCE

DIPLOMA IN APPLIED AI AND ANALYTICS

Being in an Applied AI and Analytics Course, I was exposed to various domains of computing knowledge, namely : Javascript, HTML Web Coding and Cascading Style Sheets. Overall, the content taught is extremely interesting as it is vastly different from the syllabus taught in secondary school. Besides the different lesson content, students get ample break time between lessons to catch up on work or to hang out at their favourite spots. Due to the competitiveness of the AI course, having to complete a plethora of projects within a short deadline and can get stressful at times. Some projects include building of a personal website, proposing AI-based solutions to benefit the community and many more. Despite this, the feeling of accomplishment after each project is far greater.

SCHOOL FACILITIES

CATERED TO EACH AND EVERY NEED

Singapore Polytechnic boasts a myriad of facilities that caters to every need, from gyms to study corners and vending machines. In terms of the food choices in Singapore Polytechnic, there is never a lack of options to choose from. Personally, my favourite food is the Western Chicken Chop tucked away at FoodCourt 6. If I ever need a quiet spot to study, Hilltop Haven is my first choice. It comprises of two levels and has plenty of spaces. Even during peak hours when the tables are full, there is always a cafe for me to take a quick bite and relax under the cool air-conditioning. Finally, Singapore Polytechnic offers a well-equipped gym for students like myself to keep fit and healthy. It offers a wide range of exercise equipment for usage and is able to meet each and every physical limit.

CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

THE VIDEOGRAPHY CLUB

I took up the Videography Club CCA in SP. It never fails to provide an immense amount of fun and excitement through hands-on lessons and activities in every session. The seniors of the club are extremely welcoming, and always aims to include everyone in all the activities. I must admit, I was a little worried of not being able to fit in with a new group of people. However, my worries diminished when I saw how friendly and easygoing everyone was, ready to accept any new member with open arms. In a short span of time, I learnt how to use the various functions of a camera, such as adjusting the saturation and aperture of an image to improve the quality of the picture. Moreover, I learnt how to take videos using different video techinques that produces more professional videos for viewing. Truly, the knowledge acquired through my CCA is indispensable and extremely valuable.

PROJECT WORKS

THE BEST OF POLYTECHNIC LIFE

Project Work is the highlight of my life at Singapore Polytechnic. It provides many opportunities for me to interact and get to know my classmates better. Personally, I prefer group work as the work load is equally divided and everyone has to play their part for the group to succeed. More importantly, it teaches communication and how to work amicably with people with distinct personalities. I find that through group work, tasks can be completed more efficiently with better quality of work. In contrast to individual work, group work allows for more critical thinking and ideas can be generated more quickly. As a sociable person, I enjoy collaborating with others as I find more fun through pooling ideas together and brainstorming to finalise on the most feasible one. More often than not, it can make work feel less like a chore. I always look forward to group projects in class.
